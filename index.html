Non-Electric vs Electric Bidet Attachments: What's the Difference?

What Is a Non-Electric Bidet Attachment?

A non electric bidet attachment is the simplest, most affordable entry point into the world of bidet hygiene. It mounts beneath your existing toilet seat using a plate and two bolts, connects to your cold water supply line via a T-valve adaptor, and is operated via a manual side dial that controls nozzle position and water pressure. There's no electricity involved, no outlet required, and nothing to plug in.

Non-electric attachments are ideal for bathrooms without a conveniently located outlet near the toilet, for budget-conscious buyers, and for anyone who wants a clean, effective, low-maintenance solution. They typically offer rear wash and feminine wash functionality with adjustable pressure settings. Our KMINA Ultra-Slim and Japanese Dual Nozzle models are excellent fitted non-electric options, while our range of handheld bidet sprayers — including the LOSCHEN and SonTiy — give you flexible, hands-on control.

A handheld bidet sprayer connects to your existing toilet water supply valve and mounts in a holder on the wall beside your toilet. How to Install a Bidet Attachment in Minutes

Step-by-Step Installation Guide

Installing a bidet attachment is far simpler than most people expect. Here's the process in brief:

  • Turn off the water supply valve beneath your toilet and flush to empty the tank.
  • Remove your existing toilet seat by unbolting the hinge bolts at the rear of the bowl.
  • Slide the bidet mounting bracket into position over the hinge holes and secure with the provided bolts.
  • Disconnect the cold water supply hose from the toilet tank inlet.
  • Insert the T-valve adaptor between the supply line and the tank inlet — hand-tighten firmly.
  • Connect the bidet supply hose between the T-valve outlet and the bidet attachment inlet.
  • Turn the water supply back on, check for leaks, reattach your toilet seat over the bidet plate, and you're done.

Easy Install Bidet: No Plumber Required

The entire process typically takes 10–15 minutes. Everything needed is included in the box. No cutting pipes, no soldering, no permanent modifications to your bathroom. Even electric models simply require a standard wall outlet within reach of the toilet — a short, discreet cable tuck along the skirting board is usually all that's needed.
